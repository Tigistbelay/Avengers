What It Means To Us

Essentially, ethical and sustainable fashion is an approach towards sourcing, manufacturing and designing clothes which maximizes the benefits to the industry and society at large, at the same time minimizes the impacts on the environment. We seek to operate our business within global boundaries, and to apply the highest duty of care to people within our business and supply chain. To GFI, Sustainability means to understand every element of our business responsibility for people and global. Through four key pillars Environment, Community, Responsible Workplace and Diversity & Inclusion to deliver the most positive impact on people and planet possible. Our people are empowered to bring their values to work every day, collectively own our People & Planet Positive agenda and together with our leaders prioritise this core strategic topic in all aspects of our business.

We Support The Environment

Sustainable fashion can significantly reduce consumption and waste. GFI brands prioritize long-lasting materials, so there will be less need to use new resources as well as it can help in reducing waste. Reducing the environmental impact of our operations Understanding all of the most significant impacts of our operations (e.g. transport, logistics and packaging), and implementing programs which progressively reduce that impact. DIVERSITY, INCLUSION & BELONGING

Every person has the right to be treated with dignity and respect and experience a safe workplace where they feel they belong. Equal opportunities, representation, support and accessibility for all. Fostering an inclusive environment in our daily business operations and leadership by investing in our culture and capabilities. Ensuring fair and conscious approaches to our model, supplier and brand selection Demanding accountability through policies, internal controls and targets Combating diversity discrimination internally and externally with zero tolerance
